# Privacy Policy — ExpenseTracker _Last updated: June 5, 2026_ ExpenseTracker is a personal iPhone app developed by Dani Jaffal. This policy explains what data the app handles and what it does with it. ## Short version The app collects nothing. It has no server, no account, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs. Everything you enter stays on your iPhone. ## What stays on your device Everything you create in ExpenseTracker — accounts, expenses, incomes, subscriptions, expected expenses, budgets, savings goals, debts, tags, categories, receipt photos, and your settings — is stored locally on your iPhone using Apple's SwiftData framework. None of it is uploaded, copied, or shared. If you delete the app, this data is permanently erased. If you enable Face ID, Touch ID, or device passcode lock in Settings, the authentication is handled entirely by Apple's LocalAuthentication framework. The app never sees your biometric data. ## What the app does NOT do - No analytics or usage tracking - No crash reporting to any third party - No advertising - No data sale or sharing - No account creation or login - No internet connection required for any feature - No access to your contacts, photos library (beyond the photo you explicitly pick for a receipt), location, microphone, or health data ## Permissions the app may ask for - **Notifications** — only used to remind you of upcoming subscription renewals, expected bills, and budget warnings. All notifications are scheduled locally on your device. You can deny or revoke this permission any time in iOS Settings. - **Camera / Photos** — only invoked when you explicitly attach a receipt to an expense. The image is saved inside the app's container; it isn't uploaded anywhere. ## Changes to this policy If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. ## Contact Questions or concerns: **danijaffal@hotmail.com**
